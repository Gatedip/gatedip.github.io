About me

I am working as a Project Associate at the Robotics Research Centre(RRC), IIIT Hyderabad. At RRC, My current work focuses on vision and learning in general. My previous work at RRC included working on UAV-based assessment of civil structures, where I was jointly advised by Prof. Madhava Krishna, Dr. Ravi Kiran Sarvadevabhatla and Dr. Hari Kumar Kandath.

More particularly, my current threads involve working on:
-> Object detection for adverse weather conditions
-> DodgeDrone(ICRA 2022) - Vision-based Agile Drone Flight

Prior to this, I have worked as a Software Engineer at Amdocs.

Research Interest: The intersection of Robotics, AI, and Neuroscience excites me the most, and am looking forward to working on algorithms driving the behavior of intelligent agents. Reasoning and Interpretability of such algorithms have become imperative for critical use-cases demanding high precision, and understanding this would indeed open a wide range of possibilities. I am pretty excited about the future where robots would coexist with us and this motivates me to explore this amalgamation further. My area of interests include vision, reasoning/interpretability of learning algorithms, continual learning of robots in highly unstructured environments. In addition, having interned at Swaayatt Robots which is working on Level-5 autonomy, I am fascinated with the changes that autonomous driving will be bringing to the world.

I always look forward to interesting collaborations or chats on AI. Feel free to ping me on LinkedIn or through email.

Research / Publications

SRTGAN: Triplet Loss based Generative Adversarial Network for Real-World Super-Resolution

Norwegian University of Science and Technology(NTNU, Norway)
Sardar Vallabhbhai National Institute Of Technology(SVNIT), Surat

Sept 2020 - Present

Dhruv Patel1, Abhinav Jain1, Simran Bawkar, Manav Khorasiya, Kalpesh Prajapati, Kishor Upla, Kiran Raja, Raghavendra Ramachandra, Christoph Busch. Submitted to the 7th International Conference on Computer Vision & Image Processing[CVIP] 2022.

  • We have proposed a Triplet Loss-based Patch Generative Adversarial Network(GAN) for super-resolution(SR) problem.
  • The main novelty of our work is that it uses the low- resolution images as a negative sample and differentiates high-resolution and low-resolution images on a patch- based scale. It efficiently distinguishes the foreground and background and achieves superior performance compared to other state-of-the-art methods in terms of perceptual fidelity.
    • CODE PAPER MANUSCRIPT
    Keywords: GANs, Deep Learning, Super-Resolution

    UG Project- Autonomous Agricultural Robot (TEQIP III Sponsored)

    Dhruv Patel, Meet Gandhi, Shankaranarayanan H

    Oct 2019 – June 2020 NIT Surat

    Advisor : Dr. Anand Darji (Associate Professor & Former HoD, ECED)

  • Worked on a Robot that autonomously navigate through the fields and is able to perform vision task - Crop Weed Classification which is a significant problem in the field of farming.
